Description

Sambal ikan bilis is one of the side dish for Malaysia's national dish, Nasi Lemak. However, people also eat Sambal Ikan Bilis with plain rice and with Roti Canai. Sambal ikan bilis tastes spicy due to the chillis and has that saltiness coming from the anchovies.

Some people like to make Sambal Ikan Bilis very spicy, and some prefer the spiciness to be moderate and just nice. It depends on people's spicy tolerance.

Ingredients

  • 5 medium size shallots, crushed in a pastel and mortar or blitzed in a machine
  • 1 Teaspoon of Belacan (Shrimp Paste)
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • A handful of dried chilies, seeds removed, soaked in hot water and blitzed in a machine
  • 1 heap table spoon chili paste
  • 1 Asam Keping (Tamarind Slice)
  • 200g of ikan bilis (anchovies)
